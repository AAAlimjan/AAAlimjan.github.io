At night I was reading Shannon’s biography & played little billiard on my phone. Also discovered a TED video about Elon Musk where he talk about his projects and views about the future which I found inspiring.

I think the value of beauty and inspiration is very much underrated, no question. But I want to be clear. I’m not trying to be anyone’s savior. That is not the – I’m just trying to think about the future and not be sad.

People are mistaken when they think that technology just automatically improves. It does not automatically improve. It only improves if a lot of people work very hard to make it better, and actually it will, I think, by itself degrade, actually. You look at great civilizations like Ancient Egypt, and they were able to make the pyramids, and they forgot how to do that. And then the Romans, they built these incredible aqueducts. They forgot how to do it.

I think it’s important to have a future that is inspiring and appealing. I just think there have to be reasons that you get up in the morning and you want to live. Like, why do you want to live? What’s the point? What inspires you? What do you love about the future? And if we’re not out there, if the future does not include being out there among the stars and being a multi-planet species, I find that it’s incredibly depressing if that’s not the future that we’re going to have.

20200208, 21:23, Tianjin

The quarantine is getting more & more strict. Today around 3pm her phone rang & I answered as usual since sometimes she can’t understand when Chinese speak too fast, it was from the police station near us and asked about whether we went out during this epidemic & what is our ID number balabala, then because I felt not right but I couldn’t just make up an identity & passport number in two seconds so I told them my identity & ID number.

After like one hour two policemen came, took picture of me & asked that humiliating & stupid question, such as where I work, when I came here, when I will leave & weather I leaved town during this epidemic.

Despite the systematic humiliation & discrimination, racial profiling, and cold & toxic silence of the majority of Chinese people, today I was thinking about two things: Coronavirus & Stanford Prison Experiment.

I read a quote by Heinrich Heine says ：

“Where they have burned books, they will end in burning human beings.”

I remember during the October of 2017, including me and that people burned many books that identifies or expresses our culture & identity willingly or forced. Among books I burned there was classic books that I kept as a treasure for many years including Abduhaliq’s poem collection, Abduqader Jalalidin’s essays, calligraphy books, books about culture & historic novels, because I feared that I wound be taken & set to some awful places if someone told them I have those books or if they find out in any random chance. That active book burning was a crime on my concise, and I feel the fear & surviving desire so much powerful comparing to the delusion that I usually presume that I have nobility in my internal world that I seek truth & beauty, I will sacrifice my life for science & social values.

When I thought about myself being in a confined cell or a iron bared “change or perish” kind of camp, I was afraid. I choose to burn that books & strengthen my safety & create something more than that books have contained.

They did burn books & forced us in various forms, to burn books. Shockingly, now they are burning humans indeed, without revealing the truth about them & it seems to be continued.

It feels to me that there is a kind of conservation law of evil in human beings, like when someone or some ones do evil things to a group they will be treated as such one way the other. It feels to me like justice is for the weak & might makes right. Fancy concepts like human value and rights at most of the cases are not something that we worship but one that we consume to achieve our goals.

I lost my faith in the world that I imagined in my naïve mind these hard working people of this land have a good heart & they stand for righteousness, no, their concept of truth or whatever the concept that originated from European renaissance is feed on material needs. Stomach determines the brain & imagination of living a decent human life with human dignity. People worship power, unreasonably almost all of them, like that are preprogrammed that way when the came out of the big scary factory.

Poor slaves of poor souls.
